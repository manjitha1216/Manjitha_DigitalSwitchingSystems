Multilayer Switching with Cisco Express Forwarding (CEF)

Multilayer Switching with Cisco Express Forwarding (CEF) is a technology that enhances the performance of Layer 3 switching on Cisco routers and multilayer switches. It improves the efficiency of packet forwarding by using a specialized forwarding mechanism.

Key Concepts

Multilayer switching involves the integration of Layer 2 switching and Layer 3 routing functions in a single device. CEF, on the other hand, is a packet-switching technology that optimizes the process of forwarding packets through a network.

How Multilayer Switching Works

In a multilayer switch with CEF, the device maintains a Forwarding Information Base (FIB) and an Adjacency Table. The FIB contains information about the next-hop addresses and interfaces, while the Adjacency Table holds Layer 2 information such as MAC addresses.

When a packet arrives, CEF quickly determines the appropriate next-hop based on the FIB and then uses the Adjacency Table to forward the packet at Layer 2. This process significantly accelerates packet forwarding compared to traditional routing methods.

Benefits of Multilayer Switching with CEF

The combination of multilayer switching and CEF provides several benefits, including:

  • Improved Performance: Faster packet forwarding enhances overall network performance.
  • Reduced CPU Utilization: Offloading Layer 3 switching functions to dedicated hardware reduces the load on the router's CPU.
  • Efficient Use of Resources: CEF optimizes the use of routing tables and reduces the need for constant route recalculations.

Key features of Multilayer Switching with CEF:

  • Efficient Forwarding: CEF builds and maintains a Forwarding Information Base (FIB), allowing the switch to make forwarding decisions based on Layer 3 information in hardware, resulting in faster packet forwarding.
  • Topology-based Forwarding: CEF uses a prepopulated database of the entire network topology, allowing the switch to forward packets at high rates. The longest match in the database is used to determine the correct Layer 3 destination.
  • Cisco Express Forwarding (CEF): CEF is the second generation of Multilayer Switching and utilizes specialized hardware to optimize routing information for efficient packet forwarding. It is known for its high-speed performance and scalability.

Multilayer Switching with CEF is widely used in enterprise networks to achieve efficient and high-performance routing and switching capabilities within a single device.
