Multilayer Switching with Cisco Express Forwarding (CEF)

Multilayer Switching (MLS) with Cisco Express Forwarding (CEF) is a technology that enables high-performance routing and switching in Cisco switches. It operates at multiple layers of the OSI model, combining the functions of Layer 2 and Layer 3 devices.

Key features of Multilayer Switching with CEF:

  • Efficient Forwarding: CEF builds and maintains a Forwarding Information Base (FIB), allowing the switch to make forwarding decisions based on Layer 3 information in hardware, resulting in faster packet forwarding.
  • Topology-based Forwarding: CEF uses a prepopulated database of the entire network topology, allowing the switch to forward packets at high rates. The longest match in the database is used to determine the correct Layer 3 destination.
  • Cisco Express Forwarding (CEF): CEF is the second generation of Multilayer Switching and utilizes specialized hardware to optimize routing information for efficient packet forwarding. It is known for its high-speed performance and scalability.

Unlike traditional switches, Multilayer Switches with CEF can perform routing functions at incredibly fast speeds, making them suitable for high-performance networks. This technology enhances network efficiency and reduces latency, providing a seamless experience for end-users.

Understanding how CEF operates and its integration with Multilayer Switching is essential for network administrators to optimize network performance and scalability.
